Fake news and disinformation have always existed, but perhaps it was the pandemic and all tha derived from it that made us fully understand how dangerous they can be. Valentina Petrini in "Non chiamatele fake news" develves into the phenomenon of disinformation, of the manipulation of news. She does it through research, interviews, studies, data and she also transpires a lot of curiosity and desire to understand. She talks about the Covid-19 pandemic, vaccines, climate crisis deniers, conspiracy theories, social media, immigration and politics. All of this is happening today.

This book is for the brave. If you have know anxiety, the real one, the one that blows you away because it has no logic, you should read it. You should read it with calm and breath. It is a sliding book, it can be read with curiosity and pleasure, it offers reflections and precious words that have a resonance to live. The protagonists of the book are three: Anxiety, Super parents and growing children. A trio that often walks arm in arm and that experts recognize now easily. And indee panic, anxiety and the range of anxiety disorders are the manifestation of the discomfort of our time that is knocking more than ever on the door of those concerned.
